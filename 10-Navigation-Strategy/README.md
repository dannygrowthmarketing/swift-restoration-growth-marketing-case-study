# 10 · Navigation Strategy

> Make the phone number and the right page one tap away — always.

## Executive Summary

Navigation is engineered for a panicked, mobile, high-intent user who needs to call fast or find their town/service in one move.

## Business Problem

Emergency users don't browse; they convert or leave. Deep menus, hidden phone numbers, and unclear paths to service/location pages cost calls.

## Research Findings

The current nav (About, Services, Projects, Request Quote, Contact) exposes none of the service or location depth and doesn't foreground the emergency call action.

## Strategic Recommendation

- **Primary nav:** Services (mega-menu), Service Areas (mega-menu by county→city), Resources, Reviews, About, Contact.
- **Persistent emergency CTA:** sticky header + sticky mobile call bar — "24/7 Emergency? Call (515) 784-2662".
- **Mega-menus** surface service pillars and top cities directly (shallow click depth).
- **Footer:** full NAP, hours, service list, county/city links, review snippet, social — reinforcing local + internal links.
- **Breadcrumbs** site-wide for UX + schema.

## Implementation Plan

Implement as .NET components bound to the page-type taxonomy so menus auto-populate as service/location pages are added.

## Expected Business Impact

Lower friction to call, shallower paths to money pages, and stronger internal-link distribution — directly supporting both conversion and rankings.

## KPIs

Mobile click-to-call rate, nav→service/location page CTR, bounce on emergency entries, calls per session.
