# 05 · Competitor Research

> The competitors that matter are local — and the franchises' strength is borrowed.

## Executive Summary

A tiered competitive analysis reframes the field: national franchise *corporate* domains are content benchmarks only; the businesses that actually take Swift's jobs are local franchise units and independents competing in the Map Pack and LSA.

## Business Problem

Benchmarking Swift's backlinks against SERVPRO corporate (DR 76) is a vanity exercise — it produces no actionable path. The right question is who wins the local pack and LSA in Boone/Ames/Des Moines.

## Research Findings

**Authority (Ahrefs Domain Rating, measured):**

| Competitor | DR | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Swift Restoration | 3.2 | Mid-pack among independents |
| Dream Steam | 15.0 | Strongest true independent |
| 515 Restoration | 0.0 | Beatable on fundamentals |
| SERVPRO of Ames (microsite) | 0.8 | If it ranks here, Swift already leads on authority |
| SERVPRO / ServiceMaster / Restoration 1 (corporate) | 76 / 72 / 63 | Borrowed authority — not winnable on links |

**Strategic reads:**
- **SERVPRO of Ames** is the primary threat — town-by-town location pages (Boone, Ogden, Madrid, Story City…) targeting Swift's exact footprint.
- **Dream Steam** (est. 1983, multi-city) is the strongest independent; the one real authority gap to close.
- The **Des Moines metro** (ServiceMaster by Rice, Restoration 1, 911, Rainbow, Firstcall, ProRestore) is saturated — a later-phase market.

## Strategic Recommendation

Compete on **local relevance, reviews, and LSA** in Boone/Ames, not on links. Out-build SERVPRO of Ames's templated town pages with genuinely local content (real project photos, town-specific detail). Defer the saturated metro until home turf is won.

## Implementation Plan

Feed this directly into the keyword (06), local SEO (12), and GBP (13) plans. Resolve the `swiftrestoration-us.com` ownership question before citation work.

## Expected Business Impact

A winnable competitive plan: concentrate where authority is low and intent is high, and stop spending effort where franchise corporate domains make organic parity impossible.

## KPIs

Local-pack rank vs. SERVPRO of Ames per town; LSA presence vs. competitors; review-count gap closure.
