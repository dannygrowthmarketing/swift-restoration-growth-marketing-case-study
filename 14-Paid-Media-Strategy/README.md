# 14 · Paid Media Strategy

> Fix the live defects, consolidate the structure, and open the highest-ROI channel: LSA.

## Executive Summary

Swift runs minimal, generic Search ads with a live rendering bug and no Local Services Ads. The plan fixes the defects, consolidates campaigns so Smart Bidding can learn, and prioritizes LSA as the highest-ROI emergency channel.

## Business Problem

Fragmented or generic paid search wastes budget and underperforms; the absence of LSA forgoes the single best pay-per-lead channel for emergency restoration.

## Research Findings

- Two standard Search ads under owner **Austin Moorman** ("Verified" = advertiser identity, **not** LSA).
- **Live defect:** ad copy renders a double-encoded ampersand — `extraction &amp; drying` — showing literally to searchers.
- Generic, location-less creative ("Call Now For Water Help").
- An originally-planned **eight-campaign** structure would fragment budget below Smart Bidding's conversion threshold.
- **No LSA presence** despite water damage being LSA-eligible.

## Strategic Recommendation

- **Fix the encoding bug** immediately; rewrite creative with service + town + the response-time promise + 5.0★ proof.
- **Consolidate to 2–3 campaigns** in Month 1 (others start as ad groups) so Smart Bidding gets enough conversion signal.
- **Launch Local Services Ads** once GBP verification is confirmed — pay-per-lead, top-of-page, Google-Guaranteed trust.
- **Message-match** ads to dedicated landing pages (Section 15), not the homepage.

## Implementation Plan

Week 1: bug fix + creative + consolidation + conversion tracking. Week 2–3: LSA verification + launch in Boone/Ames. Tie call tracking (Section 17) to prove cost-per-lead.

## Expected Business Impact

Lower wasted spend, faster bid-algorithm learning, and a new high-intent lead stream from LSA — measured by cost-per-qualified-call, not clicks.

## KPIs

Cost per qualified call (Search + LSA), LSA lead volume + dispute rate, conversion rate, impression share in core towns, Smart Bidding conversion volume.
