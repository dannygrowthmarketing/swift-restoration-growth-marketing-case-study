# 01 · Executive Summary

> How a DR 3.2 local operator gets engineered to win emergency leads against DR 70+ franchises.

## Executive Summary

Swift Restoration is a credible, well-reviewed, owner-operated emergency-restoration company in Boone, Iowa with a near-perfect reputation (5.0★, 15 reviews) and **almost no digital footprint to match it**. The website is a brochure: its homepage targets no keyword, it has no service pages, no location pages, and no educational content. In parallel, the business is migrating from WordPress to a custom **.NET** platform.

That combination is the opportunity. We are not optimizing an existing SEO asset — we are **building one from zero**, on a new platform, with a clean technical foundation. This engagement documents that build: the diagnosis, the architecture, the local-first growth strategy, and a sequenced execution plan tied to lead generation and ROI.

## Business Problem

A strong offline business is invisible online. Qualified emergency demand in Swift's eight-county market is being captured by national franchises and a handful of local competitors — not because they are better operators, but because they have **pages, profiles, reviews velocity, and paid presence** that Swift currently lacks. Every day of inaction is leads routed to competitors during exactly the high-intent moments (a flooded basement at 11pm) where Swift's 24/7 response would win.

## Research Findings

- **Greenfield, not optimization.** Placeholder homepage metadata; zero service or location pages; "Projects" are unlabeled photo galleries.
- **Authority is low but the field is bimodal.** DR 3.2 — yet ahead of independents like 515 Restoration (0) and the SERVPRO-of-Ames microsite (0.8). The franchise "strength" is borrowed from corporate domains (DR 63–76), unbeatable on links and therefore the wrong fight.
- **The real battleground is the Map Pack + LSA.** Swift has a 5.0★ reputation and no Local Services Ads presence — a high-ROI gap.
- **The migration is a double-edged sword:** a chance to fix everything cleanly, and a risk of losing what little equity exists if redirects and metadata aren't handled rigorously.

## Strategic Recommendation

Win **home turf first** — Boone and Ames — using the levers that convert for emergency services: a clean service/location page architecture, a Google Business Profile review engine, Local Services Ads, and tightly-matched emergency paid search. Treat the .NET migration as the build vehicle: ship the new architecture, technical SEO, and internal-linking model as part of the platform move, not as a later retrofit.

## Implementation Plan

Sequenced across the repo: technical/migration foundation (07–10) → content and local build (11–13) → paid restructure and landing/CRO (14–16) → measurement (17) → 30-day execution (18) and beyond (20). Dependencies are explicit: the migration unblocks clean templates; GBP verification unblocks LSA; tracking unblocks ROI reporting.

## Expected Business Impact

A foundation that converts Swift's offline trust into online lead flow: top-3 local-pack visibility for Boone/Ames core terms, an LSA channel feeding pay-per-lead emergency calls, and a review engine compounding local ranking and conversion simultaneously. The aim is **qualified phone calls**, not vanity rankings.

## KPIs

GBP calls & views, tracked phone calls, organic & LSA leads, local-pack rankings for core terms, review count/velocity, indexed real pages, Domain Rating. Full baseline + 30/90-day targets in [Section 19](../19-Results-and-KPIs/README.md).
