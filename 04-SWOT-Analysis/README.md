# 04 · SWOT Analysis

> Strong business, weak digital, wide-open local opportunity — with real, nameable threats.

## Executive Summary

Swift's strategic position is defined by a mismatch between operational strength and digital weakness, against a competitive field where the dangerous players are local, not national.

## Business Problem

The risk is misallocating effort — chasing franchise backlink parity (unwinnable) instead of the local-pack/LSA/review levers where Swift can win quickly.

## Research Findings

**Strengths**
- 5.0★ / 15 reviews; owner-operated local trust; 24/7 response; established 2018; genuine before/after project photos; insurance-handling competence.

**Weaknesses**
- Zero on-page keyword targeting; no service/location architecture; DR 3.2; placeholder metadata; no analytics/call tracking; duplicate-domain ambiguity (`swiftrestoration-us.com`).

**Opportunities**
- Greenfield = fast structural wins; .NET migration = clean rebuild; Boone/Ames low competition; **no LSA presence yet**; multiple content gaps unclaimed locally; review engine can compound rankings + conversion.

**Threats**
- **SERVPRO of Ames** runs town-by-town pages targeting Swift's exact towns; franchise corporate authority (DR 63–76); Des Moines metro saturation; **migration risk** (equity/redirect loss); duplicate domain potentially splitting signals.

## Strategic Recommendation

Lean into strengths (reviews, locality, speed) on the channels that reward them (GBP, LSA, local pages). Neutralize the SERVPRO-of-Ames page advantage by building better, genuinely-local town pages. De-risk the migration with rigorous redirect/metadata governance.

## Implementation Plan

Map each opportunity to an owning section: greenfield→08/09, migration→07, Boone/Ames→12, LSA→14, content gaps→11, reviews→13.

## Expected Business Impact

Effort concentrated where the win probability and revenue impact are highest, with the biggest threats explicitly mitigated rather than ignored.

## KPIs

Local-pack share vs. SERVPRO of Ames in core towns; LSA lead volume; review velocity; migration traffic-retention rate.
