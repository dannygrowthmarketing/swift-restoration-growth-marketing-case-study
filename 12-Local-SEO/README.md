# 12 · Local SEO Strategy

> Win Boone and Ames first; the metro later. Reviews + local pages + citations.

## Executive Summary

Local SEO is the core of this engagement. The plan builds genuine town pages, drives review velocity, fixes citation consistency, and tracks performance with a geo-grid — sequenced home-turf-first.

## Business Problem

For emergency services, the local pack and 'near me' results drive the calls. Swift has no location pages, inconsistent/duplicate NAP signals (the `swiftrestoration-us.com` issue), and an under-leveraged profile.

## Research Findings

- No city/county pages today; counties are unlinked text.
- **SERVPRO of Ames** owns town pages across Swift's footprint.
- A duplicate domain (`swiftrestoration-us.com`, same Boone address, different phone, 'founded 2021') risks splitting local signals — **confirm ownership first**.
- Strong but under-used review asset (5.0★/15).

## Strategic Recommendation

- Build **county → city pages** with genuinely local content (real photos, town detail, response-time promise), starting **Boone + Ames**, then Ogden/Madrid/Nevada/Story City, then Dallas/Webster, then metro.
- **Citations & NAP:** consistent NAP across GBP, Bing, Apple, Yelp, BBB, Angi, Nextdoor, chambers, IICRC — after resolving the duplicate domain.
- **Review engine** (Section 13) as the compounding lever.
- **Geo-grid rank tracking** to measure local-pack share by town.

## Implementation Plan

Templated city pages from the .NET location page-type; citation cleanup sprint; review-request workflow stood up in Month 1.

## Expected Business Impact

Top-3 local-pack presence in home-turf towns, stronger 'near me' coverage, and a defensible local moat the franchises can't easily buy.

## KPIs

Local-pack rank per town (geo-grid), GBP views/calls, citation consistency score, review count/velocity, leads by city.
