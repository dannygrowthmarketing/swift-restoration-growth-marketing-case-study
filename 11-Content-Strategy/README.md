# 11 · Content Strategy

> Localized educational content that builds authority and feeds the money pages.

## Executive Summary

Six topical clusters establish topical authority and support the service and location pages, with each asset mapped to the funnel stage and the pages it strengthens.

## Business Problem

Service pages alone rarely build the topical authority needed to outrank entrenched competitors; the leaders win partly through educational content. Swift has none.

## Research Findings

Content gaps (benchmarked vs. competitors): Insurance Claim Assistance, Emergency Water Damage Guide, Mold Prevention & Health Risks, Storm Damage Recovery, Frozen-Pipe Prevention, **Iowa Derecho & Storm Preparation** (a locally-distinctive, largely-unclaimed angle), localized case studies, and per-service FAQ.

## Strategic Recommendation

Build six pillar clusters — **Water, Mold, Fire, Storm, Insurance Claims, Commercial** — each with a pillar page, supporting articles, FAQ (with schema), and internal links to relevant service + city pages. Make **/resources/insurance-claims/** the cross-cluster trust hub (it closes deals). Re-label "Projects" as **located, captioned case studies** ("Basement Flood Cleanup — Ames, IA").

## Implementation Plan

Prioritize Insurance hub + Emergency Water guide + located case studies + per-service FAQ first (highest lead/trust impact); layer seasonal pieces (frozen pipes, derecho) ahead of their demand windows. Full mapping in the companion keyword/content workbook.

## Expected Business Impact

Higher topical authority, more entry points for high-intent informational queries, stronger internal linking to money pages, and trust content that lifts conversion.

## KPIs

Cluster rankings, assisted conversions from resources → service pages, FAQ rich-result coverage, case-study engagement.
