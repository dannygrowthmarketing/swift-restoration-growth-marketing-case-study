# 06 · Keyword Strategy

> Prioritized by revenue potential, not search volume.

## Executive Summary

Because the site targets nothing today, every commercial keyword is a gap. The strategy maps a complete service × location keyword universe and prioritizes by likelihood of generating a qualified emergency call.

## Business Problem

Chasing the highest-volume terms (Des Moines metro) first would pit a DR 3.2 site against saturated competition and waste the early window. Volume ≠ value for emergency services.

## Research Findings

- **Current targeting:** effectively none; current impressions are brand/navigational.
- **Service gaps:** standalone Mold, Storm, Sewage, Commercial, and Emergency lines have no pages.
- **Priority tiers (P1→P4):** P1 emergency intent ("emergency water damage restoration Boone/Ames", "flooded basement cleanup", "burst pipe cleanup", "24 hour water damage restoration") → P2 core service pages → P3 city+service → P4 informational/funnel.
- **Local priority:** Boone and Ames lead **despite lower volume** than Des Moines, due to lower competition and higher conversion likelihood.

## Strategic Recommendation

Build P1/P2 money pages and the Boone/Ames P3 pages first; layer informational P4 content to support topical authority. Use estimate **bands** now; replace with exact MSV/difficulty once a data tool is connected.

## Implementation Plan

Full keyword universe, local matrix, prioritization, and clusters are delivered in the companion workbook (`Keyword Research & SEO Opportunity Analysis`). Pages map 1:1 to the architecture in Section 08 and sitemap in Section 09.

## Expected Business Impact

A roadmap where the first pages built are the ones most likely to ring the phone — compounding as location and content layers are added.

## KPIs

Rankings for P1 emergency terms in Boone/Ames; leads attributed to service vs. location pages; coverage of the service × location matrix.
