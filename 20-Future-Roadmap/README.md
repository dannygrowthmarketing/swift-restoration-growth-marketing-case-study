# 20 · Future Roadmap

> From home-turf foundation to regional authority.

## Executive Summary

Beyond Month 1, the plan scales the proven home-turf playbook across the full eight-county footprint, builds the content clusters and citation profile, and expands paid into the metro — compounding reviews and authority over 60–180 days.

## Business Problem

Early wins must be scaled deliberately; expanding into the saturated Des Moines metro too early would dilute focus before the foundation pays off.

## Research Findings

Boone/Ames wins create a repeatable template; content clusters and citations build the authority needed to contest larger markets; LSA and reviews compound throughout.

## Strategic Recommendation

**60 days:** remaining service pillars (Mold, Fire, Storm, Commercial, Sewage); Story/Boone-county town pages; insurance + emergency content hubs; citation profile built out.

**90 days:** Dallas/Webster county+city pages; seasonal content live (frozen pipes, derecho) ahead of demand; review milestone (45+); local-link/citation acquisition.

**180 days:** Des Moines metro expansion (Polk/Dallas) once home turf is held; full content-cluster maturity; LSA scaled across towns; authority growth via sustained local links; quarterly reporting on cost-per-qualified-lead and market share.

## Implementation Plan

Iterate the location page-type and content templates; reinvest based on the cost-per-lead data from Section 17/19; revisit competitive position quarterly.

## Expected Business Impact

A durable local moat (reviews + local pages + LSA) in home turf, expanding into the metro from a position of strength — sustained qualified-lead growth at improving efficiency.

## KPIs

Matrix coverage (services × counties), metro local-pack share, review milestones, referring-domain growth, cost-per-qualified-lead trend.
